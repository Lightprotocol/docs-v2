No. The c-token SDK follows similar patterns to SPL.

<Accordion title="Can c-Token accounts hold SPL tokens?">
Yes. c-Token accounts can hold tokens from c-Mints, SPL mints, or Token 2022 mints.
SPL tokens can be converted to c-Tokens and back.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What happens if my c-Token account runs out of rent?">
The account is automatically compressed.
Your tokens are preserved as a compressed token account (rent-free). The account decompresses automatically when you interact with it again.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What is the difference between c-Mint, c-Token, and compressed token?">
- **c-Mint**: Compressed account representing a token (like SPL mint). Rent-free.
- **c-Token**: Solana account that holds token balances of c-mints, SPL or Token 22 mints. Uses sponsored rent.
- **Compressed token**: Compressed account storing token data. Rent-free, for storage and distribution.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How do I query c-Token and compressed token balances?">
Standard Solana RPC works for c-Token accounts (Solana accounts). Compressed token balances require the ZK Compression RPC.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can I convert between c-Tokens and SPL tokens?">
Yes. SPL tokens can be deposited into c-Token accounts and withdrawn back to SPL token accounts via the `transferInterface` method.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What are compressed tokens good for?">
- Token distribution at scale
- Storage of inactive tokens
</Accordion>
